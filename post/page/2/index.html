H drivetrain researching

Pros of the H-drive Due to its additional support systems, which make it more robust and balanced than other drive trains, it is first and foremost significantly more stable and simple to manage than the X-drive.This implies that it can move around during the autonomous phase more steadily. The wheel in the centre gives it greater manoeuvrability than the tank drive. The extra wheel serves as a pivot, enhancing the H-drive’s ability to turn with greater agility.

Understanding the X Drive

Our team created a tank drive to understand the basics of a drivetrain in order to create more complex drivetrains in the future. A Tank Drive is a simple drive train consisting of c-channels, 4 traction wheels or 2 traction and 2 omni wheels. The omni wheels that may be on the robot are locked since locking an omni wheel will optimize turning. How we built the X-drive We researched the x-drive and its structures.

Mecanum drivetrain prototype

The mecanum drive was the first ever drive we built with the vex parts. First, we created a simple tank drive but after that we added different sensors and the mecanum wheels. The sensors we added were the optical sensor, the gps sensor, and the rotation sensor. The mecanum drive is not our actual robot and is only used to testing. For example, right now we are testing the gps sensor for the vex autonomous skills challenge.
